Road to the Civil War
Secession of South Carolina
December 1860
What was it?

Due to the victory of Abraham Lincoln in the presidential election, South Carolina was the first state to secede from the federal Union. The South Carolina Declaration of Secession was issued by the South Carolina government in which they explain their reasonings for secession

Why?

The South Carolina Declaration of Secession explains that the secession was due to the government failing to uphold its obligations to South Carolina. South Carolina asserted that one of the main issues was that some states did not enforce the Fugitive Slave Act and they believed the government has a role in abolishing slavery. The election of Abraham Lincoln ultimately pushed them to succeed because he was planning to abolish slavery.

Who?

John C. Calhoun led the South Carolina secession. He served South Carolina as a representative, secretary of war, vice president, and senator.

How did it lead to the Civil War?

The secession of South Carolina prompted many more southern states to secede from the Union. The ten other states that seceded, along with South Carolina formed the Confederate States of America which fired on Fort Sumter, igniting the American Civil War.
