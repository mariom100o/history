Road to the Civil War
Battle of Fort Sumter
April 1861
What was it?

The Battle of Fort Sumter was the bombardment of Fort Sumter by the South Carolina militia. The United States Army returned gunfire and then eventually surrendered, where the Confederacy won the battle.

Why?

President Abraham Lincoln announced plans to resupply the fort, which prompted General P.G.T Beauregard to bombard Fort Sumter.

Who?

P.G.T. Beauregard led the South in the Battle of Fort Sumter. He was the Confederacy’s first brigadier general who ordered the first shots in the Battle of Fort Sumter.

How did it lead to the Civil War?

The Battle of Fort Sumter triggered the Civil War because it was the first time Confederates fired on a Union fort. That battle led to many more during the Civil war.
