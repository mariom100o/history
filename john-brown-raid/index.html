Road to the Civil War
John Brown's Raid on Harpers Ferry
October 1859
What was it?

John Brown’s Raid on Harpers Ferry was an effort made by John Brown to initiate a slave revolt in the Southern states. John Brown along with a small group of abolitionists raided a federal arsenal in Harpers Ferry, Virginia, trying to arm enslaved people and destroy slavery itself. From the raid, seventeen people were killed and many of the abolitionists, including John Brown, were jailed facing charges of murder.

Why?

John Brown wanted to weaken the slavery institution, and completely eliminate slavery. He would do this by arming enslaved people and starting a slave revolt.

Who?

John Brown initiated the raid in hopes of completely eliminating slavery itself. Because of this, he was charged with murder and eventually executed for his crimes.

How did it lead to the Civil War?

Even though the raid was unsuccessful in completing John Brown’s wishes it made sure there would be no accommodation between the north and south, basically making the civil war inevitable. It also worked as a precedent for the Civil War, modeling the actual United States Civil War.
