Road to the Civil War
Lincoln-Douglas Debates
August 1858
What was it?

The Lincoln Douglas Debates were a series of seven debates held between Abraham Lincoln and Stephen A. Douglas during the Illinois senatorial campaign. The issues discussed in the debates were mainly about the issues of slavery.

Why?

The seven debates were held for the 1858 Illinois senatorial campaign.

Who?

Abraham Lincoln, the Republican Party candidate for the United States Senate from Illinois debated with Democratic Party candidate, Senator Stephen Douglas.

How did it lead to the Civil War?

The debates strengthened the divide between the parties and Lincoln portrayed Douglas as upholding a “wrong”. The debates gained Lincoln some favor for the upcoming presidential election, in which he will win, which also pushed the United States closer to civil war.
