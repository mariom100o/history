Road to the Civil War
Compromise of 1850
September 1850
What was it?

The Compromise of 1850 was five bills passed by Congress in response to political disagreements regarding the status of territories that were acquired through the Mexican-American War. The Compromise of 1850 amended the Fugitive Slave Act and abolished the slave trade in Washington, D.C. Along with this, it also resolved a boundary dispute between New Mexico and Texas, where a territorial government was established in New Mexico.

Why?

There were still several large slavery issues and conflicts between the north and south, which called for another compromise. The Compromise of 1850 was established to try and avert the dissolution of the Union.

Who?

Henry Clay came out of retirement to work out the Compromise of 1850, dying just two years later. He also established the Missouri Compromise thirty years earlier, when he was a senator.

How did it lead to the Civil War?

The Compromise of 1850 included the Fugitive Slave Act, which allowed southerners to come to retrieve slaves from a free state. This means slaves, and even free black people were not free in a free state. This lead to further conflict and divide between the north and the south. This also created a divide between the parties. Finally, the Compromise of 1850 overturned the Missouri Compromise, which left the overarching issue of slavery unsolved.
