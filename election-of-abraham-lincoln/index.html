Road to the Civil War
Election of Abraham Lincoln
November 1860
What was it?

During the sixteenth presidential election, Abraham Lincoln faced Douglas, Breckinridge, and Bell. Abraham Lincoln won the national popular plurality, the popular majority in northern states where slavery was already abolished, and the national electoral majority, which only comprised of northern electoral votes.

Who?

Abraham Lincoln was a lawyer in Kentucky and Whig representative to Congress. Eventually, he gained a seat in the United States Senate before winning the presidential election.

How did it lead to the Civil War?

Because Abraham Lincoln was directly opposed to the expansion of slavery, his election did not settle well win many pro-slavery citizens. His election also initiated the secession of many southern states from the Union. His large unpopularity among southerners and the secession of southern states brought the United States extremely close to civil war.
