Road to the Civil War
Wilmot Proviso
August 1846
What was it?

The Wilmot Proviso was an amendment to President K. Polk’s appropriation bill regarding the funding of newly acquired territories. Along with changing Polk’s appropriation, it also prevented the expansion of slavery into territories acquired from Mexico due to the Mexican-American war. This amendment did not pass as it was defeated in the Senate.

Why?

It was designed to remove slavery from land that was acquired by America in the Mexican-American war. Since David Wilmot did not want the creation of more pro-slave states.

Who?

David Wilmot, who was a Pennsylvania Democratic Congressman, issued the Wilmot Proviso. He was against the expansion of slavery into other states.

How did it lead to the Civil War?

Even though the amendment did not become law, it still divided the Democratic Party between northerners and southerners since there were disagreements between ideas regarding slavery in newly acquired territories. Since the south heavily relied on slavery, the Wilmot Proviso was seen as an attack on them, inching the United States closer to the civil war.
