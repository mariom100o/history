Road to the Civil War
Kansas-Nebraska Act
May 1854
What was it?

The Kansas-Nebraska Act repealed the Missouri Compromise and created two new territories, Kansas and Nebraska. It also allowed these states to vote on whether slavery was legal or not. The act declared that slavery is legal in these areas.

Why?

The bill was created to organize the territory of Nebraska which would eventually become Kansas, Nebraska, Montana, and the Dakotas.

Who?

Senator Stephen Douglas of Illinois proposed the bill to organize the Nebraska Territory. He hoped that his idea of popular sovereignty in the bill would resolve the issues over slavery in the United States.

How did it lead to the Civil War?

The Kansas-Nebraska Act practically eliminated the Missouri Compromise, which has been holding the United States from breaking out into Civil War for thirty years. It allowed slavery in territories in which it was once banned. This strengthened the conflict between the north and the south. The act also lead to the period called Bleeding Kansas, which even further pushed the United States closer to Civil War.
