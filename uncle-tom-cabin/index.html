Road to the Civil War
Uncle Tom's Cabin
March 1852
What was it?

Uncle Tom’s Cabin was an anti-slavery novel. This novel narrated the story of an enslaved man, showing him as a noble person. This portrayed the idea that slavery is unjust by conflicting with the idea that black people did not have as much emotional capacity as white people.

Why?

Harriet Beecher Stowe was prompted by the tightened Fugitive Slave laws to write Uncle Tom’s Cabin.

Who?

The author of the novel was Harriet Beecher Stowe. She was an abolitionist and was most likely motivated the spread her ideas on black people

How did it lead to the Civil War?

The novel had a major influence on how American citizens viewed slavery. The novel countered ideas that portrayed black people had limited emotional capacities. This caused many people to become against slavery, which further widened the gap between the north and south while also strengthening the north. The novel also helped gain favor for Abraham Lincoln, whose election also brought the United States closer to the Civil War.
