Road to the Civil War
Fugitive Slave Act
September 1850
What was it?

The Fugitive Slave act was one part of the Compromise of 1850 in which southerners were allowed to retrieve runaway slaves from free states. This act also requires northerners to return any slaves to their masters in the south if found, completely undermining the ideas of the northerners.

Why?

Henry Clay was pressured by the southern politicians, leading to the inclusion of the Fugitive Slave Act in the Compromise of 1850 in order to please the south.

Who?

Henry Clay established the Fugitive Slave Act as part of his Compromise of 1850. This was an effort to tone down the conflict between the north and the south.

How did it lead to the Civil War?

The fugitive slave act was one of the most controversial acts that were passed in the United States. By requiring the citizens in free states to capture and return runaway slaves, the act completely undermines the thoughts and beliefs of the northerners. It also creates the opportunity for free black people to be falsely captured back into slavery. This lead to a major uproar in the northern states and created extreme conflict between the north and south, getting the United States very close to the Civil War.
