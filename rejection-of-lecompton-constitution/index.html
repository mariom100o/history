Road to the Civil War
Rejection of the Lecomption Constitution
January 1858
What was it?

The Lecompton Institution was the second proposed constitution for the new state of Kansas. It was created by pro-slavery advocates who included provisions to protect slavery in the state and it excluded free black people from the bill of rights. This constitution never passed.

Why?

The number of anti-slavery advocates greatly outnumbered the number of pro-slavery advocates in the vote, rejecting the Lecompton Institution 10,226 to 138.

Who?

Not one specific person made proposed the constitution, but it was proposed by several pro-slavery advocates in Lecompton Kansas.

How did it lead to the Civil War?

The Lecompton Institution cause many northern Democrats to convert to the Republican party, and the rejection caused much turmoil in the Democratic Party. It also caused much more friction and conflict between the two parties, getting the United States closer to civil war.
