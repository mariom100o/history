Road to the Civil War
Sumner-Brooks Affair
May 1856
What was it?

The Sumner-Brooks affair was an altercation between Representative Preston Brooks, who was a pro-slavery Democrat from South Carolina, and Senator Charles Sumner, who was an abolitionist Republican from Massachusetts. Preston Brooks used a walking cane to physically attack Charles Sumner, beating him nearly to death.

Why?

During Charles Sumner’s anti-slavery speech, he verbally attacked Preston Brooks’ first cousin once removed. This filled Brooks with anger leading him to attacking Sumner with a walking cane.

Who?

Preston Brooks was an American politician who was a member of the United States House of Representatives from South Carolina. He was a Democrat who was strongly in favor of slavery. He is most widely known for attacking Charles Sumner in the United States Senate chamber.

How did it lead to the Civil War?

The beating significantly contributed to the division of pro-slavery and anti-slavery people. The use of violence was showcased by Preston Brooks eventually led to the Civil War.
